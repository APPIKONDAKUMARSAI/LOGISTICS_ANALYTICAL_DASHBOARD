

# 🚚 Logistics_Analytical_Dashboard

## Project Title: US Supply Chain & Logistics Analytical Dashboard

### Short Description:
An interactive Looker Studio dashboard designed to transform raw commercial shipping and order data into actionable supply chain insights. This business intelligence tool provides deep visibility into regional demand distribution, product category volumes, consumer segments, and geographic order trends across the United States.

---

### Tech Stack:
* **BI Platform:** Looker Studio (formerly Google Data Studio)
* **Data Sources:** US Commercial Logistics Dataset (CSV/Google Sheets)
* **Geospatial Mapping:** Google Maps Integration
* **Visualization Elements:** Custom Donut Charts, Stacked Bar Charts, Line Trends, and Geo-maps.

---

### Data Source:
The dashboard processes transactional shipping records featuring:
* **Geographic Attributes:** US Regions (West, East, Central, South) and major distribution Cities.
* **Product Taxonomy:** 17 unique product categories (Binders, Paper, Storage, Phones, etc.).
* **Customer Demographics:** Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office segments.
* **Financial Metrics:** Order quantities, tracking IDs, and a custom profit slider margin range ($-170.8$ to $635.5$).

---

### Features / Highlights:
* **Regional Market Share:** Clear breakdown of order density showing major hubs in the **West (31.5%)** and **East (30.1%)**.
* **Product Demand Density:** Multi-color stacked horizontal bar charts mapping exactly how product volumes (led by Binders and Paper) scale across different regions.
* **Customer Segmentation Profile:** Interactive donut charts proving that **Consumers (50.9%)** represent the bulk of order operations, followed by Corporate buyers (28.6%).
* **Geospatial Hotspots:** A live US point-map displaying order concentrations to instantly spot shipping densities and logistics clusters.
* **Chronological Trend Lines:** Tracking total order quantities across specific shipping dates to monitor seasonal logistics volatility.
* **City-Level Tracking:** Granular vertical volume bars isolating primary high-volume shipment hubs like New York City, Los Angeles, and Philadelphia.
